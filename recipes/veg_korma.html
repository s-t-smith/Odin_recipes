Vegetable Korma

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound carrots
  • 1.5 pounds of gold potatoes
  • 1 cup green beans
  • 14 ounces of canned diced tomatoes
  • 1 white onion
  • 3 cloves of garlic
  • 3 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon of powdered ginger
  • 1 tablespoon of garam masala
  • 1 tablespoon of curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon of coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon of turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon of cayenne pepper
  • 1 to 2 teaspoons of salt
  • 1 cup raw cashews
  • 13.5 ounces of coconut milk (full fat)
  • 1 cup frozen peas

Equipment:

  • Blender or food processor
  • Knife and a cutting board
  • Strainer or colander
  • Medium soup pot
  • Large soup pot

Steps:

  1. Gather the dry spices (garam masala, curry powder, coriander, turmeric, cayenne, ginger, salt) in a bowl and set them aside.
  2. Boil a few cups of water in a medium soup pot.
  3. Place the cashews in a bowl and cover them with the boiling water. Allow them to soak while you prep the vegetables.
  4. Peel the carrots and chop them into slices about 1/2 inch thick.
  5. Peel the potatoes and cut them into chunks about 1 inches wide.
  6. Put the carrots and potatoes in the medium pot, cover them with water and bring them to a boil. Boil the vegetables until the potatoes can be easily pierced with a fork (about 15 minutes).
  7. Once th potatoes are finished, drain the vegetables and set them aside.
  8. Chop the ends off the green beans and cut them into pieces about an inch long.
  9. Drain the canned tomatoes and pour them into the blender.
  10. Peel and quarter theh onion, add it to the blender with the tomatoes.
  11. Crush and peel the garlic cloves, then add them to the blender.
  12. Blend the tomatoes and onion until a smooth puree is made.
  13. Add the olive oil to the large pot and place the pot over medium-high heat.
  14. Add the tomato puree to the large pot and bring it to a boil.
  15. Add the dry spice mix to the puree and stir for 2 to 3 minutes, allowing the spices to "bloom" in the mix.
  16. Reduce the heat under the large pot to bring the puree to a simmer (letting the puree simmer low for a while usually helps bring out the flavors, so no need to rush).
  17. Drain the cashews and add them to the blender.
  18. Pour the coconut milk in the blender with cashews and blend them into a fine mix.
  19. Pour the cashew mix into the large pot with the puree.
  20. Add the peas and green beans to the large pot and cook until the vegetables are tender.
  21. Add the carrots and potatoes, stirring regularly and letting everything simmer up to the same temperature.
  22. Remove from heat and serve over basmati rice or naan.
