Beef Empanadas

This receipe does not include ingredients or steps for empanada pastry shells. Store-bought will suffice.

Ingredients:

  • 350g ground beef (80% lean, 20% fat)
  • Olive oil (enough to cover the bottom of a pan)
  • Garlic (about four cloves)
  • 1 white onion, diced
  • Chicken stock (about a cup)
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • Parsley
  • Cayenne pepper
  • Cumin
  • Oregano
  • Paprika

Equipment:

  • Knife and a cutting board
  • Deep pan or pot and a good stirring spoon
  • Baking sheet and parchment paper

Steps:

  1. Add olive oil to the deep pan or pot and place over medium heat.
  2. Add ground beef to hot oil. Optionally, add salt and pepper to taste.
  3. Stir beef and cook until a layer of au ju is present in the bottom of the pan.
  4. Add diced onion, bell pepper, crushed or minced garlic.
  5. Stir the beef and vegetable mixture occasionally, cooking until the onion pieces are slightly clear.
  6. Add dry seasonings to the mixture (to taste) and reduce heat.
  7. If needed, add chicken stock to the pan to deglaze and increase moisture.
  8. Cook over low heat until reduced. If the mixture is too wet, it will be difficult to keep in the pastry later.
  9. Remove mixture from heat and allow it to chill. Preheat an oven to 375F.
  10. When mixture is cool to the touch, add an amount to empanada shells, fold the shell closed and crimp the edges.A fork is useful for crimping, but this can also be done by hand.
  11. Place filled and crimped shells onto a baking sheet covered with parchemnt paper and set the sheet in the oven at 375F for 25 to 35 minutes.
  12. Remove the empanadas from the oven and use a fork to punch a few holes in the tops (this will help the mixture inside cool before serving).
  13. Serve as finger food, or with utensils and sauce!
